# Pseudo Corp - Code of Conduct

> **Effective Date:** 2025-01-01
> **Owner:** Office of the General Counsel / Ethics & Compliance
> **Classification:** Internal
> **Applies to:** All directors, officers, employees, contractors, interns, and
> any AI agent or automation acting on behalf of Pseudo Corp.
>
> This Code is fictitious, created solely to demonstrate policy-enforcement
> tooling. "Pseudo Corp" is not a real company.

The Code of Conduct expresses the principles that guide how we work. It
complements - but does not replace - the Employee Handbook and applicable law.
Where the Code and the Handbook both address a topic, follow the stricter rule.

---

## 1. Purpose & Core Values

1.1 Our purpose is to help teams ship software with confidence, responsibly.

1.2 We act on five core values: **integrity, respect, customer focus,
accountability, and stewardship.** Every decision and every action - including
those we delegate to tools and AI agents - should be testable against these
values.

---

## 2. Integrity & Honest Dealing

2.1 We are honest and truthful in all business dealings, records, and
communications.

2.2 We never falsify, alter, or misrepresent information - including expense
reports, timesheets, contracts, customer records, or product claims.

2.3 We compete fairly and do not engage in fraud, deception, or manipulation.

---

## 3. Conflicts of Interest

3.1 We avoid situations where personal interests conflict - or appear to
conflict - with the interests of Pseudo Corp.

3.2 We disclose any actual or potential conflict to our manager or to Ethics &
Compliance and recuse ourselves from related decisions.

3.3 We do not use our position, or company information, for personal gain.

---

## 4. Anti-Bribery & Gifts

4.1 We never offer, give, accept, or authorize bribes, kickbacks, or any
improper benefit, directly or indirectly, to or from any person - whether a
public official, customer, supplier, or other party.

4.2 Modest, customary business gifts and hospitality may be acceptable within
the limits published by Ethics & Compliance. We never accept or offer gifts that
are cash or cash equivalents, that are intended to influence a decision, or that
could create a sense of obligation.

4.3 Any gift or hospitality above the published threshold must be disclosed and
approved before it is given or accepted.

---

## 5. Respect & Dignity at Work

5.1 We treat every colleague, candidate, customer, and partner with dignity and
respect.

5.2 We do not engage in harassment, bullying, discrimination, or intimidation,
in any form or channel. This applies to messages we write ourselves and to
messages we cause an AI agent to draft or send on our behalf.

5.3 We value diversity and do not tolerate discrimination on the basis of race,
national origin, ethnicity, religion, age, gender, sexual orientation, marital
or family status, disability, or any other protected characteristic.

5.4 Hiring and personnel decisions are based on job-related criteria only
(Employee Handbook §4).

---

## 6. Protection of Company Assets & Information

6.1 We protect Pseudo Corp's physical, financial, and information assets, and
use them only for authorized business purposes.

6.2 We safeguard confidential and personal information in accordance with its
classification (Employee Handbook §5) and never disclose it without
authorization.

6.3 We protect the security of our systems and devices and do not weaken,
disable, or circumvent security controls (Employee Handbook §6).

---

## 7. Responsible Use of AI & Automation

> **Permitted:** using AI agents for authorized work, with automated guardrails that inspect and block policy-violating prompts and tool calls before they execute.
> **Prohibited:** using an agent to bypass an approval workflow, a security control, or a separation of duties; instructing an agent to act in a way that would be a violation if performed by a person.

7.1 We embrace AI and automation to work more effectively, but we remain fully
accountable for the actions of any AI agent or automation that acts on our
behalf or on behalf of Pseudo Corp (Employee Handbook §1.3).

7.2 AI agents must comply with this Code and the Employee Handbook exactly as a
human employee would. An action that would be a policy violation for a person is
a policy violation when performed by an agent.

7.3 Before an AI agent is permitted to take a **consequential action** - such as
sending external communications, making or authorizing payments, signing
contracts, modifying systems or software, handling confidential or personal
data, or publishing externally - the integration must be gated by automated
guardrails that inspect the agent's prompts and tool calls against these
policies and block non-compliant actions before they execute.

7.4 We do not use AI agents to circumvent an approval workflow, a security
control, or a separation of duties. Speed and convenience are never a
justification for skipping a required control.

7.5 We do not instruct - explicitly or implicitly - an AI agent to act in a way
that we know would be a violation if we performed the action ourselves.

---

## 8. External Communications & Social Media

8.1 Only authorized spokespersons speak for Pseudo Corp externally. Posts to
official company social-media accounts require Corporate Communications approval
(Employee Handbook §11).

8.2 In personal social media, employees should be clear that they speak for
themselves and not for Pseudo Corp, and must not disclose confidential or
non-public information.

---

## 9. Records, Accuracy & Financial Integrity

9.1 We create accurate, complete, and timely business records.

9.2 We record time worked, expenses incurred, purchases made, and contracts
entered into truthfully and in the correct systems, and we never backdate or
mischaracterize them (Employee Handbook §2, §7, §8, §9).

9.3 Customer and personal data is used only for authorized purposes
(Employee Handbook §12).

---

## 10. Reporting Concerns & Non-Retaliation

10.1 If you see, or reasonably suspect, a violation of this Code, the Handbook,
or the law, you must report it - to your manager, HR, Ethics & Compliance, or
the confidential reporting channel.

10.2 Pseudo Corp prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in
good faith or participates in an investigation.

---

## 11. Compliance, Waivers & Consequences

11.1 Compliance with this Code is mandatory and is a condition of engagement.

11.2 Waivers of this Code for executive officers or directors require approval
of the Board; other waivers require approval from Ethics & Compliance and must
be documented.

11.3 Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including
termination, recovery of improper gains, and, where applicable, legal action.

---

## 12. Acknowledgement

12.1 We each have a personal responsibility to understand and follow this Code,
to ask when we are unsure, and to help our colleagues - and our tools - do the
same.

*End of Code of Conduct.*
